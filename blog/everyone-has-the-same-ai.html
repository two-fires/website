AI & Marketing

Everyone Has the Same AI. That's the Opportunity.

AI has made average content free and infinite. Which is exactly why craft and distinctiveness matter more than they ever have.

13 July 2026

Open your feed. A large share of it was written by the same handful of AI models, prompted in much the same way, saying much the same things in much the same voice.

Your competitors have the exact tools you have. So does everyone else in your category. So does everyone outside it.

Most businesses see that as a threat. Read it again. It is the opening.

What AI actually changed

AI made competent content free and infinite. That is not a small thing. But when everyone can produce competent content instantly, competent stops being an advantage.

It becomes the floor. The baseline. The price of entry.

The whole internet now sounds like a polite, capable robot that has read everything and believes nothing. Technically fine. Completely forgettable.

What still cannot be copied

A genuinely distinctive point of view. Real customer insight, earned rather than assumed. Brand assets people recognise on sight. Judgment about what to say, and the harder discipline of what to leave out.

AI can execute all of it beautifully. It cannot originate any of it. That gap is where every remaining marketing advantage now lives.

Built from craft, not gimmick

This is the whole reason we exist in the shape we do.

The engine does the production. It is fast, tireless and very good. But the fires, thirty years of brand work, decide what is worth producing in the first place, and make sure it sounds like you and no one else.

Using AI is not the advantage anymore. Everyone is doing that. Having something worth amplifying is the advantage.

The reframe

In a market where everyone holds the same tools, the businesses that win are the ones with a foundation worth multiplying.

That starts with craft, not software. It is where we begin every time.
